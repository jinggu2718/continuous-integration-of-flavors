# Matcha White Chocolate Chip Shortbread 
based on recipe from [Just One Cookbook](https://www.justonecookbook.com/green-tea-white-chocolate-cookies/) 

## Ingredients 
1. 1 1/2 cup flour, packed 
    - I like to scoop flour directly from the bag without fluffing. the original recipe calls for 2 cup flour, and the conversion between spoon-and-leveled and packed is 4:3 
2. 3 tablespoon matcha powder 
    - please use unsweetened matcha powder. available at most Asian groceries 
3. 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened 
    - butter can be softened in the microwave. place butter in a microwave-safe bowl and heat on half power. it is totally okay if the butter ends up partially melted, simply stir it back together until smooth 
    - the softened butter should not give much resistance when stirred, but still holds shape 
4. pinch of salt 
5. 1 cup powdered sugar 
6. 2 egg yolks, room temperature 
    - eggs are easier to separate when cold 
    - the remaining egg whites can be frozen and later used for [financiers](https://github.com/jinggu2718/continuous-integration-of-flavors/blob/main/orange_chocolate_financier/recipe.md)
    - egg yolks need to be at room temperature because we are mixing them with softened butter, and need to prevent them from partially re-solidifying the butter 
7. white chocolate chips, to taste 
    - white chocolate chips help offset the bitterness from the matcha. I usually use ~1/2 cup 

## Instructions 
1. in a medium bowl, mix the flour and matcha powder until well combined 
2. add the softened butter and powdered sugar in a large bowl, mix with a fork until well combined and fluffy 
3. add the egg yolks, mix until even 
4. add the flour-matcha to the butter mixture, mix until mostly combined; add the white chocolate chips, mix until just combined and the white chocolate chips are close to evenly distributed 
    - this should result in a soft dough 
    - first part of the mixing can be done with the fork, but hands will be needed to bring the dough together 
5. divide the dough into two halves, shape each into a log, wrap with plastic wrap and chill in the fridge for at least 2 hours 
    - the dough will solidify when chilled, making it possible to slice neatly in the future step, and help the cookies hold shape while baking 
6. preheat oven to 300 F with convection on; line two baking trays with parchment paper 
    - baking with convection toasts the white chocolate chips, adding a nice caramel flavor and a bit of crunch; the original recipe calls for baking at 350 F 
7. slice the chilled cookie dough log (one at a time) into ~1/3 inch thick slices with a sharp knife, arrange onto the lined baking tray, leaving ~2 inch between slices 
    - we will have to cut through the white chocolate chips, so use a sharp knife 
    - cut with a smooth downward motion instead of a sawing motion to avoid crumbling white chocolate chips 
8. bake for 15 minutes
9. remove from the oven. let cool on the baking tray for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to let cool completely 
    - the freshly baked cookies will be quite fragile, so be careful when transfering them 
    - the cookies might taste dry and powdery when they are warm, because the butter in them have not yet "set". they will taste better once fully cooled
