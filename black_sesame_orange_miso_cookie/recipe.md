# Black Sesame Orange Miso Cookie 
based on recipe from [healthy nibbles](https://healthynibblesandbits.com/orange-black-sesame-cookies/) 

## Ingredients 
1. 1 1/2 cup flour, packed 
    - the original recipe calls for 2 cups + 1 tablespoon flour. the ratio between spoon-and-leveled and packed is about 4:3 
2. 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 
3. 1 cup granulated sugar 
4. zest from 1 orange 
5. 3/4 cup butter softened 
    - butter can be softened in the microwave by placing it in a microwave safe bowl and heating in 15 seconds intervals on half power, stirring between each interval to help it soften evenly 
    - it is completely fine if the butter ends up partially melted, simply stir it back together until smooth. refridgerate if needed 
6. 1/3 cup brown sugar 
7. 1/3 cup black sesame paste 
    - available at most asian grocery stores. look for the unsalted unsweetened ones 
8. white miso paste, to taste 
9. vanilla extract, dash 
10. 1 egg, room temperature 
    - we are using softended butter, so the egg needs to be room temperature to avoid partially re-solidifying the butter 

## Instructions 
1. combine the flour and baking soda in a medium bowl and mix well 
    - mixing the flour and baking soda first help distribute the baking soda evenly without overmixig the dough 
2. combine the orange zest and granulated sugar in a small bowl and mash together 
    - the sugar will turn slightly yellow 
    - mashing the zest and sugar together help release the oil from the zest, which strengthen the orange flavor and help distribute it throughout the cookie 
3. add the brown sugar and the sugar zest mixture to the softened butter, mix with a fork until fluffy 
4. add the black sesame paste, miso, and vanilla extract to the butter mixture, mix until well combined, taste and adjust 
    - everything in the mixture at this point is safe to eat raw; the egg will be added later 
    - I like a slightly salty cookie, so I use about 4 tablespoon of miso; strongly recommend starting with less and then taste and adjust, as different misos have different level of saltiness 
5. add the egg, mix until well combined 
6. add the flour mixture, mix until just combined 
7. refridgerate for at least 3 hours 
    - this makes the dough easier to work with in following steps 
8. preheat oven to 325 F with convection on 
9. line a baking sheet with parchment paper 
10. portion out the chilled dough and roll out to 1.5-1.75 in diameter balls, place on the baking sheet, leaving at least 3 in in between; flatten the dough balls slightly by pressing down with palm 
    - the cookies do not spread that much, so we need to flatten it 
11. bake for ~14 minutes 
    - the cookies should brown slightly around the edge; the dough is off-black, but the edges will take on a red tint when they brown 
    - the cookies will rise a bit in the middle and develop fine cracks, but will deflate to mostly flat onces cooled 
12. remove from oven, let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer carefully to a cooling rack and let cool completely 
    - the cookies will be fragile while they are still warm
