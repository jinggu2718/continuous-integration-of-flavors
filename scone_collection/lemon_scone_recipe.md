# Lemon Scone 
based on recipe from [Sally's Baking](https://sallysbakingaddiction.com/scones-recipe/)

## Ingredients 
### Scone 
1. 1 1/2 cups all purpose flour, unsifted 
    - the original recipe calls for 2 cups spooned and leveled. I usually just scope directly with measuring cup from the flour bag, the 1 1/2 cup is given for this method 
2. zest from 2 lemons 
    - zest only the lemon part of the skin. the white part of the skin is bitter and should not be used 
3. 1/2 cup granulated sugar 
    - can adjust to taste 
4. pinch of salt 
5. 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder 
6. 1/2 cup unsalted butter 
    - cut into ~1/2 inch thick slices, then return to fridge or freezer to keep cold 
7. 1/2 cup heavy cream 
8. 1 large egg 
    - straight out of the fridge 
9. dash of vanilla extract 

### Icing
10. ~1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
11. ~1/2 cup powdered sugar 

## Instructions
1. place the lemon zest and sugar into a large bowl, mash together with a fork
    - mashing the zest with sugar help release the oil from the zest 
2. add the flour, sugar, salt, baking powder into a large bowl and mix until even
3. mix the cold/frozen butter to the flour mixture; working quickly, break the butter into very small pieces (about pea-sized) using fingure tips. put the mixture in the fridge to keep it from melting 
    - if the weather is warm, consider putting the mixture in the fridge for 5-10 minutes half way through to stop the butter from melting 
    - butter contains water, and creates steam in the oven, and is what makes the scone flaky. puff pastries function on the same principle. However, if the butter pieces are warm, they could melt and leak out of the dough before they can create steam pockets. so our end goal is to maintain as many solid butter pieces as possible when the scones go into the oven
4. in small bowl, mix the heavy cream, egg, vanilla extract 
    - mix well to break up the egg white 
5. take the flour mixture out of the fridge, drizzle the cream-egg mixture over it in batches while mixing, bring the mixture together just until it can form a rough dough ball
    - err on the side of too little liquid at the begnning: stop adding liquid when the mixture looks like part dough and part wet sand. try to bring the dough together before adding more liquid 
    - avoid over-working the dough, to minimizing gluten development and keep the scones tendere
6. turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface, press into a rectangle ~4x8 in, cut into halves along the short side to end up with two squares, stack; press into 8x4 in rectangle, cut along short edge, stack again, and press again 
    - this help to give scones more layers by stacking the butter pieces and then pressing them thinner, again, similar to puff pastries
    - this step can also be done with proper pastry folds, but I find this easier 
7. divide the dough into 2 equal halves, and adjust each to roughly circular shape, and press to ~1 in thick disks, wrap in cling film and refridgate for at least 1 hour 
    - reshape dough by smoothing over the edges with hands. maintain orientation of the dough so the work we did in step 5 do not go to waste
8. pre-heat oven to 350 F with convection on 
9. take the dough disks from the fridge, one at a time, cut into 8 wedges, arrange on a baking tray lined with parchment paper, and brush the top of each lightly with heavy cream 
    - heavy cream help with colors and makes the top crunchier 
10. bake for 18 minutes
11. remove from oven, let cool on the baking tray for a few minutes, transfer to wire rack to cool completely 
    - scones taste best freshly baked and still warm, FYI
12. after the scones are fully cooled, make lemon icing by combining powdered sugar with lemon juice in a small bowl, mix with a spoon until smooth, adjust with powdered sugar or lemon juice if needed 
    - add lemon juice a little bit at a time, the icing texture changes very quickly with lemon juice quantity 
    - the icing should be opaque, and when poured should hold the wrinkle briefly. similar in texture to a very soft whipped cream 
13. apply the icing either by pouring over, dipping, or drizzling 
    - warm scones tends to melts and absorb the icing 
    - for neat drizzling effects, put the icing into a small ziplock bag, cut one corner, and drizzle over the scones
